# Crypto Investigation Risk Indicators

This document lists common risk indicators reviewed during blockchain investigations.

---

## Wallet Behavior Risk

- Newly created wallet with high-value transfers
- Rapid movement of funds through multiple wallets
- Repeated failed transactions
- Unusual transaction timing
- Interaction with many unknown contracts
- High-value outgoing transfers shortly after receiving funds

---

## Contract Interaction Risk

- Interaction with unverified contracts
- Suspicious approval transactions
- Unlimited token approvals
- Interactions with contracts tied to scams or exploits
- Failed contract calls
- Unknown function calls

---

## Token Transfer Risk

- Large token transfers to unknown wallets
- Rapid token movement after receiving funds
- Transfers involving low-liquidity or suspicious tokens
- Repeated transfers between related wallets
- Token dusting activity

---

## DeFi / dApp Risk

- Interaction with unaudited protocols
- Bridge activity involving suspicious wallets
- Liquidity removal after token promotion
- Repeated swaps through obscure tokens
- Possible mixer or obfuscation behavior

---

## Investigation Notes

Risk indicators do not automatically prove malicious behavior. They are signals that require additional review, supporting evidence, and context.
